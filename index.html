Quantum Computing Foundations

Quantum computing operates within complex Hilbert space, where qubits exist in superposition states rather than deterministic binary states. The quantum state is defined as:

|ψ⟩ = α|0⟩ + β|1⟩

The squared magnitudes |α|² and |β|² represent measurement probabilities, constrained by normalization: |α|² + |β|² = 1. This probabilistic amplitude structure enables exponential state scaling.
